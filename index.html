Description: Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, a gas giant with no solid surface. It is known for its prominent bands of clouds and a massive storm called the Great Red Spot.

Notable Features: Great Red Spot, multiple moons (including the largest, Ganymede), and a strong magnetic field.

Description: Mars, often called the "Red Planet," is the fourth planet from the Sun. It is known for its reddish appearance due to iron oxide on its surface. Mars has been a target for exploration due to its potential for past or present life.

Notable Features: Olympus Mons (largest volcano), Valles Marineris (large canyon), and polar ice caps.

Description: Earth's only natural satellite, the Moon, is the fifth-largest moon in the solar system. It has a significant impact on Earth's tides and has been a subject of fascination throughout human history.

Notable Features: Craters, lunar seas, and varying surface terrains.

Description: Saturn is the second-largest planet, known for its stunning ring system made up of ice particles, dust, and rocks. It is a gas giant with a lower density than water, meaning it could float in a giant bathtub.

Notable Features: Spectacular ring system, diverse moons, and a hexagonal storm on its north pole.

Description: The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system. It is a hot, glowing ball of gas that provides heat and light to the planets through nuclear fusion reactions in its core.

Notable Features: Core (nuclear fusion), photosphere (visible surface), solar flares, and sunspots.
